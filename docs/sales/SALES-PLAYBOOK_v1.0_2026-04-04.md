### 6.2 Stage Details

#### Stage 1: Prospecting (1-2 Days)
**Goal:** Identify qualified opportunities

**Activities:**
- Research upcoming elections
- Identify campaign leadership
- Check party websites and news
- LinkedIn prospecting
- Referrals and networking

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Election within 6-12 months
- [ ] Campaign budget identified (>₦200M)
- [ ] Decision maker identified
- [ ] Contact information obtained

---

#### Stage 2: Qualification (3-5 Days)
**Goal:** Confirm fit and buying intent

**BANT Qualification:**
- **Budget:** Does campaign have adequate budget?
- **Authority:** Are we talking to decision makers?
- **Need:** Is there a clear pain point?
- **Timeline:** Is there urgency (election date)?

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "Tell me about your current campaign operations."
2. "What challenges are you facing with field coordination?"
3. "How do you currently manage your voter database?"
4. "What's your timeline for the election?"
5. "Who else is involved in this decision?"

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] BANT confirmed
- [ ] Pain points documented
- [ ] Decision process understood
- [ ] Demo scheduled

---

#### Stage 3: Discovery (5-7 Days)
**Goal:** Deep dive into needs and requirements

**Activities:**
- Stakeholder interviews
- Current state assessment
- Pain point documentation
- Success criteria definition
- Technical requirements gathering

**Key Questions:**
- "Walk me through a typical day in your campaign."
- "Where do you see the biggest inefficiencies?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this problem?"
- "What does success look like for you?"

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Stakeholder map complete
- [ ] Technical requirements documented
- [ ] Success metrics defined
- [ ] Proposal scope agreed

---

#### Stage 4: Demonstration (1-2 Weeks)
**Goal:** Show solution fit

**Demo Types:**
- **Executive Overview** (15 min): ROI, high-level features
- **Voter Management** (30 min): Database, segmentation, analytics
- **Field Operations** (30 min): Mobile app, GPS, coordination
- **Intelligence** (20 min): Sentiment, monitoring, reports

**Best Practices:**
- Customize demo to prospect's needs
- Use their data if possible
- Address objections in real-time
- Include success metrics
- Schedule follow-up immediately

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Demo completed with all stakeholders
- [ ] Technical fit confirmed
- [ ] Pricing discussed
- [ ] Proposal approved to send

---

#### Stage 5: Proposal (1 Week)
**Goal:** Present commercial offer

**Proposal Contents:**
- Executive summary
- Scope of services
- Pricing breakdown
- Implementation timeline
- Success metrics
- Terms and conditions
- Case studies

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Lead with Professional tier (middle option)
- Include ROI justification
- Offer payment terms (50/50)
- Mention add-ons (training, data)

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Proposal delivered
- [ ] Receipt confirmed
- [ ] Questions answered
- [ ] Negotiation scheduled

---

#### Stage 6: Negotiation (3-7 Days)
**Goal:** Reach mutually agreeable terms

**Common Negotiations:**
- Price (refer to discount authority matrix)
- Payment terms
- Scope adjustments
- Timeline changes
- Support levels

**Tactics:**
- Understand their constraints
- Trade, don't give away
- Emphasize value, not price
- Know when to walk away

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Pricing agreed
- [ ] Terms accepted
- [ ] Legal review initiated
- [ ] Signature scheduled

---

#### Stage 7: Close (1-2 Days)
**Goal:** Signed contract and deposit

**Closing Activities:**
- Contract preparation
- Legal review
- Signature collection
- Initial payment
- Kickoff scheduling

**Exit Criteria:**
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] Deposit received (50%)
- [ ] Welcome email sent
- [ ] Onboarding scheduled

## 8. Competitive Positioning

### 8.1 Competitive Landscape

**Traditional Political Consultancies:**
- **Examples:** Local campaign consultants
- **Strengths:** Relationships, experience
- **Weaknesses:** No technology, intuition-based
- **Win Theme:** "Experience + Data beats Experience alone"

- **Examples:** NationBuilder, NGP VAN
- **Strengths:** Features, brand recognition
- **Weaknesses:** No Nigerian context, USD pricing, no offline

- **Examples:** Salesforce, HubSpot
- **Strengths:** Customizable, familiar
- **Weaknesses:** Not purpose-built, expensive customization
